About Me

I am a postdoctoral research fellow in the Data Science Lab (DaSL) at the Fred Hutch Cancer Center. I work on developing training materials for data science education.
I received my PhD in Statistics from the University of Washington in 2024, advised by Amy Willis. My dissertation focused on statistical methods development for applications in microbiome science. I am excited about developing statistical software to assist researchers in analyzing their microbial ecology and genomics data. I enjoy teaching statistics, both to college students and to researchers from other fields.

I received my undergraduate degree in Statistics from Amherst College in 2018.
