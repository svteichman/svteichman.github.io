About Me

I'm a fifth-year PhD student in the Department of Statistics at the University of Washington. My research interests include statistical methods for the analysis of microbiome data and phylogenetic trees. I am advised by Amy Willis. I am excited about developing statistical software to assist practitioners of molecular microbial ecology and genomics to analyze their data. I enjoy teaching statistics at all levels. I have also done work with Tyler McCormick on methods to model wage heterogeneity.

I received my undergraduate degree in Statistics from Amherst College in 2018.
